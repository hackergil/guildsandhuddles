Business Guilds

Guild: A medieval association of craftsmen or merchants, often having considerable power

Business Guild: An association of people for mutual aid or the pursuit of a common goal

  • Knowledge domain
  • Community of enthusiasts
  • Set of tools and practices

Connection: Software as craft

  • Learn a craft
  • Master-Apprentice relationships (Mentoring)
  • Social networking (community)
  • Proper ways to perform the craft (Practices)

Why?

  • Learn from others
  • Share ideas
  • Standardize ways of working
  • Mentor and be mentored
  • Explore new technologies, tools, practices
  • Improvement of the company domain, and the industry

Corporate Huddles

  • Meetings that allow for quick, horizontal decision making among peers
  • Peers talking to peers about a central discussion
  • Small version of a business guild
  • Facilitate formation of Tribes

Why?

  • Less micromanagement
  • More cross-functional (and informal) communication
  • Communication facilitator (user requirements, documentation, QA definition, tech, etc)
  • Knowledge sharing

How?

  • Find alike minds
  • Get together (Brown bag lunches, sponshorship, meetups, etc.)
  • No politics
  • Code of conduct
